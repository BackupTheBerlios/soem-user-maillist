>
> Has anyone here ever implemented etherCAT across a TCP/IP network?

Yes.

> Would this be supported by SOEM?

Not easily.

I tried to do this to get SOEM working on Windows. I may try again just 
for fun, but I ended up writing my own ethercat master because I wanted 
a few other things handled differently.

Using UDP with ethercat works great. But if your goal is to route 
datagrams through intermediate machines, you'll have other issues to 
overcome. Ethercat wants to communicate using UDP broadcasts. It's 
tricky to get routers to handle this kind of traffic properly.

Another problem is that UDP datagrams enter the ring and come back with 
exactly the same UDP headers. These are seen as "martians" by many 
firewalls and the linux kernel. In older versions of linux they are 
dropped unconditionally. In newer kernels, you can enable them by 
turning off rp_filter on the interface. Windows accepts them without any 
fuss.
